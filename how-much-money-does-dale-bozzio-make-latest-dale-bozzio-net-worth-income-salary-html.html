Dale Frances Bozzio (née Consalvi; March 2, 1955) is a progressive rock and new wave vocalist from the United States. She is most known for her work with Frank Zappa and as co-founder and lead singer of the 1980s pop/new wave band Missing Persons. Bozzio recorded three albums and one EP throughout her solo career. She appeared in two of Zappa’s main works, Joe’s Garage and Thing-Fish, while working with him. In 1980, Bozzio and former Zappa musicians Warren Cuccurullo and Terry Bozzio co-founded Missing Persons (her husband from 1979 to 1986).          She supplied lyrics to the band in addition to being the primary vocalist. Missing Persons released one EP and six LPs, one of which, Spring Session M, was certified gold. Bozzio was signed to Prince’s Paisley Park label when Missing Persons dissolved in 1986, and her debut solo album, Riot In English, was published. Simon Simon, the band’s first single, charted well. Missing Persons was formed by Bozzio, who continues to perform the band’s repertoire at locations across the United States. She has also continued to perform as a solo artist and has joined in reunions of the original band on a regular basis. Bozzio joined with Cleopatra Records in 2014 and released his new studio album, Missing In Action.
