Xavien Howard | Career

In Indiana, Howard attended the 2016 NFL Scouting Combine. After completing the combined drills, he finished 12th among defensive backs in the short shuttle.

In addition, Xavien also took part in Baylor’s pro day. With scouts and spokesperson from all 32 NFL teams watching, Howard chose to redo most of his workouts and improve on all of his combined numbers.

Finally, the Miami Dolphins drafted him in the second round of the 2016 NFL Draft with the 38th overall pick.

However, Howard suffered a knee injury during organized team activities and minicamp on the final day and was assigned on the physically unable to perform list.

Despite missing the first three preseason games, he was named the team’s second starting cornerback for the regular season.

In 2018, after an exceptional performance, Xavien made his first Pro Bowl appearance and was chosen to his first second-team All-Pro team.
