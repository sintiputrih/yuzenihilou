House, Cars, and Yacht

Killiney Ireland House

In the suburb of Dublin, a beautiful 6000 square feet home of Bono, “Temple Hill” is situated.

The Manor-style house was bought by the singer in the 1980s and substantially renovated over the years.

The area where this mansion stand is called Dalkey, a posh seaside neighborhood with many famous celebrities around.

The house serves a beautiful view of Killiney Bay, up to Dalkey with its medieval church and Martello Tower. The house sits in such a peaceful area where the family can enjoy the sounds of birds in the trees and the sea crushing off the rocks below.

There is an additional home of 2500 square feet adjacent to their mansion which the singer bought later. The two homes are connected via a corridor.

Outside is a huge gate surrounding the mansion as this beautiful home also has become a tourist destination.

As a tribute to Bono’s stardom and talent, people have etched their favorite song from the band onto the copper gate.

Villa in the South of France

In the Cote d’Azur region of southern France, there is a beautiful villa owned by the musician that sits right on the beach.

The luxurious beachfront villa boasts gorgeous views of the Mediterranean Sea and lies directly below the highway and main train station.

The lavish home is fully private and includes four floors and has grounds back onto the railway station Exe bord du Mer. Its living area rises five meters up from the beach.

This luxurious mansion of Bono is built for both comfort and style where he is neighbors with his band’s guitarist, The Edge.

Manhattan apartment

The singer owns an apartment at The Sam Remo apartment building in Manhattan which he bought for $15 million.

This apartment located in the north tower of the building was previously owned by the late Steve Jobs and Bono bought it later.

The apartment was on the top floor (27th floor) with 3,500 square feet of interior space and 1300 square feet of terrace.

San Remo was built in 1930 and includes 136 apartments which all feature spacious rooms and lavish finishes. After becoming a co-op in the 1970s, the apartments have been renovated with modern appliances as well as thermal window glass.

The building has all standard amenities required and is located close to Central Park and cultural attractions as the Museum of Natural History.

Yacht

Bono also owns a 140 ft long yacht The Cyan on his he spent more than 25 million dollars.

This prized possession of the singer includes six cabins that can accommodate 12 guests. Each of the rooms is constructed with limed oak and mahogany and features plasma screens, DVD players, and satellite TVs.

Other features of The Cyan are Jacuzzi, Swim platform, and various water sports such as tenders, sea bobs, and wave runners. Furthermore, it also carries fishing equipment, snorkeling gear, and a kite-surfer.

The yacht can cruise at the maximum speed of 19 knots and its weekly charter price has been mentioned as $204,000 to 255,000.

Charity

Bono has always maintained a high profile, in both music and philanthropy. For his efforts in music and his humanitarian work, he was awarded an honorary knighthood by Queen Elizabeth II.

In 2005, he was also named as Time Person of the Year and made a Commander of the French Order of Arts and Letters.

The singer was listed in the Forbes-Generous Celebrity list for helping Debt AIDS Trade Africa (DATA) on their cause of eradicating and stopping the spread of the fatal disease in Africa as he has always advocated for canceling the third-world debt.

Bono has contributed to Hurricane Katrina relief, Wildlife Conservation Society, Not On Our Watch, Mulago Positive Women’s Network, Make Poverty History, Food Bank For New York City, Elton John AIDS Foundation, and Charity Projects Entertainment Fund, to name a few.

Moreover, he has made a personal donation of $50,000 to One in Four Ireland, an organization fighting against sexual abuse.

The band U2 has outspoken on several causes and worked for society as well. They have protested nuclear power plants, supported democratic movements, and much more.

Furthermore, in 2020, the band has donated €10 million to source the much-needed personal protective equipment (PPE) for healthcare workers in Ireland.

    Career

    Bono was born as Paul David Hewson in Dublin, Ireland on 10th May 1960 and later nicknamed “Bono Vox” by his friend which translates into Good voice in Latin.

    Born as the second son of Brendan Robert “Bob” Hewson and Iris Hewson, he has an elder brother named Norman Hewson. Bono went to Glasnevin National School and later attended Mount Temple Comprehensive School.

    The legendary singer was just sixteen when he founded U2 after seeing an advertisement on his high school’s bulletin board. The band’s name was coined as “U2” in 1977.

    The band reached international acclaim in 1980 when then landed a record deal with Island Records. Since then, they have released 14 studio albums, 3 compilation albums, 15 video albums, 72 music videos, and 83 hit singles.

    They’ve been included in the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and have won 22 Grammys, more than any other group.

    Personal Life

    The singer married businesswoman and activist Alison Hewson on 21st August 1982, at the Church of Ireland.

    Alison is also greatly involved in humanitarian activities alongside her husband, serving as an equal partner in their endeavors. The couple is blessed with four children together; two daughters and two sons.
