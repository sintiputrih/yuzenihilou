The top 5 Boxing Southpaws of all time

August 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Left-handed boxers, also known as southpaws, have always held a special place in the sport of boxing.

Their unorthodox stance and style often present unique challenges to their opponents, making them a force to be reckoned with in the ring.

Throughout the history of boxing, several southpaw fighters have left an indelible mark on the sport with their skill, charisma, and lasting impact.

1. Manny Pacquiao

Manny "Pac-Man" Pacquiao, hailing from the Philippines, is widely regarded as one of the greatest boxers of all time, regardless of stance.

As a southpaw, Pacquiao's speed, power, and relentless aggression have made him a formidable opponent in the ring.

His ability to move swiftly and unleash rapid combinations has earned him numerous titles across multiple weight divisions, solidifying his legacy in the sport.

Pacquiao's notable victories over legendary opponents such as Oscar De La Hoya, Miguel Cotto, and Juan Manuel Marquez have cemented his status as an iconic southpaw in boxing history.

2. Pernell "Sweet Pea" Whitaker

Pernell "Sweet Pea" Whitaker, known for his exceptional defensive skills and slick southpaw style, is revered as one of the most elusive and technically proficient boxers of all time.

His mastery of footwork, head movement, and counterpunching made him a nightmare for his adversaries.

Whitaker's Olympic gold medal win in 1984 and his undisputed lightweight championship reign showcased his unparalleled boxing prowess, leaving an enduring impact on the sport.

3. Marvin Hagler

Marvin "Marvelous" Hagler, a dominant force in the middleweight division during the 1980s, possessed a powerful southpaw stance coupled with relentless aggression.

Hagler's iron chin, formidable punching power, and indomitable will inside the ring made him a true boxing legend.

His epic battles against opponents like Thomas Hearns, Roberto Duran, and Sugar Ray Leonard have etched his name in boxing history, solidifying his status as one of the greatest southpaws to grace the sport.

4. Chris Eubank

Chris Eubank, a British boxing icon, showcased an eccentric yet effective southpaw style during his illustrious career. Known for his flamboyant personality and showmanship, Eubank's unorthodox boxing technique, coupled with his exceptional reflexes and punching accuracy, propelled him to numerous world titles and memorable victories. 

5. Zab Judah

Zab "Super" Judah, a skilled southpaw boxer from Brooklyn, New York, made a lasting impression in the world of boxing with his speed, power, and technical prowess.

Judah's lightning-fast hand speed and slick southpaw tactics enabled him to achieve world championship success in multiple weight classes.

His memorable bouts against the likes of Floyd Mayweather Jr. and Cory Spinks showcased his resilience and tenacity, solidifying his position as one of the premier southpaws in boxing history.

 

Ultimately, the influence of southpaw boxers on the sport of boxing cannot be understated. Their unorthodox stance, exceptional skill sets, and memorable performances have contributed to the rich tapestry of the sport.

The top 5 boxing southpaws of all time, including Manny Pacquiao, Pernell Whitaker, Marvin Hagler, Chris Eubank, and Zab Judah, have left an indelible mark on boxing history, serving as an inspiration to aspiring boxers and captivating fans worldwide.
