Meet Alexander Vega, Lizzie Sobinoff Boyfriend

Alexander Vega first came to light as the mystery man Lizzie Sobinoff was dating during the Christmas holiday 2021. At the time, the reality star uploaded some IG pictures with her new boyfriend but hid his face with heart-eyes emojis to keep his identity under wraps.

It seems that Alexander and Lizzie preferred to maintain a low-key profile regarding their relationship. And from what little the star shared over her IG, the pair appeared to be happiest and at the best moment of their lives.

Before dating Alexander, Lizzie was married to Sib Guilhaus over Married at First Sight. However, the pair immediately called it quits within a year in 2020. 

“We are both respectful of each other and are still involved in each other’s lives. We’ve cherished each other and have grown together. We would like to send love and well wishes to all,” they shared identical statements at the time.

Also, before marrying Sib, Lizzie was partnered with Sam Ball when MAFS fans first met her in 2019. But Sam then went on to infamously have an affair with co-star Ines Basic, and Lizzie didn’t find love that season.

Now that Married at First Sight season 14 is on its way, fans are wondering if Lizzie would still try her luck again on the show despite already having a boyfriend. However, if you ask us, the chances are very slim. 

Did you know: Lizzie had already put a black and white picture of Alexander over her IG story a month before he got introduced in Dec 2021.

Alexander Vega Job

Alexander Vega is a field service engineer. He has worked in the information technology and services industry, and has garnered skilled in “Computer Repair, Servers, Software Installation, Networking, and Cisco Systems Products.”

As of 2021, he was working as a field service technician at Micromax Computers, Central Coast for almost 10 years. Also, at the time he worked the same job at three other companies — ASI Solutions, Service NSW, and Lexmark International Inc for over 9 years each.

Reportedly, he joined Service NSW as a field service technician in Jan 2013 and was promoted to field support technician four months later.

Before that, Alexander was a field technician of Amtek Corporation from Oct 2014 to April 2021.

Also, he has worked in the field service for companies like Telstra, Bytecraft Systems, and Jehbco Manufacturing Pty Ltd.

In case you want to know, a Service Engineer in Australia earned around AU$69 thousand in 2021

As for his education, Alexander received his information technology certificate III from the Northern Sydney Institute – TAFE NSW in 2010. Then he went on to get a diploma in information technology from the same college in 2018.
